# Genomic adaptation of _Achromobacter_ spp. in patients with primary cilliary dyskinesia

## About

Over the past decades increasing pathogen resistance to antibiotics is posing a threat to public health. One genus of such opportunistic pathogens is _Achromobacter_ spp. that is inherently resistant to antibiotics, increasingly causing infections in people with primary ciliary dyskinesia (PCD), leading to often fatal complications. It was found that _Achromobacter_ spp. display antimicrobial resistance and virulence factors, with active selection in DNA maintenance and protein synthesis genes driving adaptations to the respiratory environment in PCD patients via mutation and regulation, that could be used as potential targets when developing novel therapeutic strategies.
